General information about applying for Keck time can be found on the Keck Observatory Website here. This is the Keck website that includes people to contact if you have questions, forms, observing considerations, and information for Keck proposals for other institutions.


Available Time

A total of 5 Keck nights are available for 2021B (August 1, 2021 - January 31, 2022) .

   Keck  I:   3 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 1 grey, 1 bright
   Keck II:   2 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 0 grey, 1 bright

The number of nights on each telescope and lunation distribution is mostly fixed, however there may be some flexibility and this will be decided by the STACK and sent to Caltech for approval. Any flexibility will be mainly in the lunation distribution on each telescope, with much less flexibility, if any, in the number of nights on each telescope.


COVID-19 impacts

Due to the likely continued impacts on the observatory and the observing community by COVID-19:

- WMKO is advising proposers that there may be currently unforeseen impacts on their ability to execute the allocated schedule. In particular, specialty modes (e.g. PyWFS, ESI IFU, VV coronagraphs) may be unavailable for operation.

- Some instruments and modes of instruments like NIRSPAO and ESI may be scheduled in limited blocks or campaigns to minimize load on support crews.

- In order to reduce the support load on observatory staff, partial night allocations may be limited. Proposers should consider whether full night allocations can meet their science goals. Please see here for more details.

Also note that depending on the situation in Victoria, work permits may be required to be able to observe on campus. Please make sure you allow adequate time to apply for work permits or special permissions when required.

There is also an option for At-Home (pajama mode) Observing:

- Keck will support up to two observers for at home observing: one primary and one secondary observer. Those two individuals may help other team members get set up, but Keck support will be limited for additional observers.

- At home observing requires the installation of software which only runs on linux and macOS operating systems.

- Observers using at home observing should plan to work with Keck staff to install and test the software several days ahead of their run to allow time for troubleshooting.

Director’s Discretionary Time (DDT)

In accordance with the Caltech and Swinburne collaborative agreement for Keck, the COO and SUT Directors have agreed to set aside, between them, 1-2 nights each semester from their Director’s allocation to support highly ranked CIT-SUT collaborative proposals. These proposals should seek to develop new and innovative scientific collaborations esp. those with long term strategic potential to have high scientific impact and/or increase the research capabilities of each organisation. The following additional selection criteria will be used for evaluation:

(1) High-risk high-reward pilot proposals for new science
(2) pilot for strategic collaborative project
(3) significant ongoing collaboration potential
(4) evidence of existing significant ongoing collaboration

Applicants may use up to 0.5 additional pages in the proposal template to discuss how their proposal meets all or any of these selection criteria. The TACs will make a recommendation on these to the Directors. The final decision on these allocations will be made by the COO and SUT Directors jointly. They reserve the right not to make an allocation if proposals do not reach a high enough bar.

Please read the Applicant Policy and Procedure for more details and contact the Technical Secretery if you require any further clarifications on the DDT process.

Laser Guide Star Observations

Laser Guide Star (LGS) time is now available for both the KI and KII telescopes.
Because half nights of laser science combined with non-laser science are almost as resource intensive for Keck as whole LGS nights, proposers are encouraged to match half night LGS allocations with other LGS allocations. The STACK will note that the Keck I and Keck II lasers will not be scheduled on the same night unless it is for a program doing simultaneous observations on both telescopes and the STACK will take care to distribute allocated laser nights across the semester to avoid over-burdening the laser support team in any given month.

AO Upgrades: The new LGS near-IR tip/tilt system on Keck I, TRICK is now available. Refer to Keck AO page and this document.



Submitting a Proposal:

(1) Check your eligibility in the Applicant Policy and Procedure document.

(2) Check instrument availability and latest information. Details of Keck instruments available in 2021B are given on the Keck Instrument Availability page. Instrument specifics and relevant proposal information can be found on the Keck Instruments & Facilties page.

(3) Prepare your proposal application. A single application has two components - an Online Cover Sheet and a Project Outline.

   Online Cover Sheet       KECK ONLINE COVER SHEET
The Keck Observatory requires an online cover sheet that is available at the above link. You will need to create a Keck user account if you do not already have one. Please make sure to include ALL collaborators on this cover sheet, not just those from Swinburne. As part of the cover sheet, observers are instructed to indicate which observing run dates during the semester are preferred (P), acceptable (A), or not acceptable (X) for their programs. It is to the observer's advantage to be as liberal as possible in specifying acceptable dates. A PDF version of the Keck cover sheet is to be sent with the project outline. Using your web browser's print facility, this cover sheet can be saved as a PDF file or postscript and converted to pdf using the UNIX ps2pdf command.

   Project Outline                STACK PROPOSAL FORM
All applicants will need to download the 2021B version of the STACK proposal form above. Instructions and page limits are included as comments in the proposal. Additionally, you can use the version in Overleaf . Use instructions here to copy the overleaf project.

(4) Combine the two PDF files into one file. This can be done in a variety of ways such as using the program combinepdfs or dragging and dropping in preview for Macs or by using the UNIX pdfmerge command. Proposals must use the naming convention PIlastname_semesterAB_proposalnumber.pdf (e.g. Lastname_2021B_W180.pdf) and emailed to the STACK technical tecretary KeckTS@swin.edu.au by the deadline with the email subject heading PIlastname_semesterAB_proposalnumber. The Keck proposal number can be found at the top of the Keck Cover Sheet and is issued once you fill out and save an initial version of the Cover Sheet.

(5) For PIs that have been awarded time in the past, you will need to describe your outcomes on the Keck Publication Page here.

Proposal Outcomes

The STACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-October (semester A) and late-April (semester B). Approved proposals are not confirmed until Keck is able to successfully schedule the time (which is not guaranteed). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).


Remote Observing/Eavesdropping - Fully remote observing is now operational! Keck Remote Control Room

Swinburne now has the capability to control the instruments and observations at Keck completely remotely from campus (room Luton Lane 2L003), i.e., mainland-only observation mode. Duplicate network connections, a backup ISDN line, duplicate (mirrored) computers, and an uninterrupted power supply are a few of the features added to ensure trouble-free observations. The fully remote capability complements Swinburne's long-standing remote eavesdropping mode. For remote observing assistance, please contact Marianne Girard at mgirard@swin.edu.au

Those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities for either fully remote or eavesdropping mode, must contact Keck's Mainland Observing Coordinators and Marianne Girard at mgirard@swin.edu.au. This must be done 4 weeks in advance of your run for mainland fully remote mode and 2 weeks in advance for mainland eavesdropping mode.


Financial Assistance

We expect and encourage staff to use the remote facilities, however funding is available to travel to Keck for exceptional circumstances (i.e., first time using an instrument, first time to Keck). The Swinburne Advisory Committee for Keck (SACK) proposes that applications for Keck Observing Support (KOS) funds should be made by PIs of successful proposals once they have determined the amount of funding to be available from other sources, particularly the Faculty Research Grant Schemes and other ARC sources. Please contact the Deputy Director for funding requests.
