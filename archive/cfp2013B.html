Available Time

A total of 8 Keck nights are available for 2013B (August 1 2013 - January 31 2014).

   Keck  I:   4 nights are available with lunation distribution 2 dark, 1 grey, 1 bright
   Keck II:   4 nights are available with lunation distribution 2 dark, 0 grey, 2 bright

The number of nights on each telescope and lunation distribution is mostly fixed, however there may be some flexibility and this will be decided by the STACK and sent to Caltech for approval. Any flexibility will be mainly in the lunation distribution on each telescope, with much less flexibility, if any, in the number of nights on each telescope.

Laser Guide Star Observations

Laser Guide Star (LGS) time is now available for both the KI and KII telescopes. The restriction on the number of available LGS nights has been lifted. Keck would like scientific demand from all institutions to determine both the number of nights and the distribution of nights between Keck I and Keck II.

Because half nights of laser science combined with non-laser science are almost as resource intensive for Keck as whole LGS nights, proposers are encouraged to match half night LGS allocations with other LGS allocations. The STACK will note that the Keck I and Keck II lasers will not be scheduled on the same night unless it is for a program doing simultaneous observations on both telescopes and the STACK will take care to distribute allocated laser nights across the semester to avoid over-burdening the laser support team in any given month.


Submitting a Proposal

(1) Check your eligibility in the STACK policies and Procedures document.

(2) Check instrument availability and latest information. Details of Keck instruments available in 2013B are given on the Keck Instrument Availability page. Instrument specifics and relevant proposal information can be found on the Keck Instruments & Facilties page. For example, LRIS and DEIMOS slitmasks are due at least 4 weeks prior to the observations.

(3) Prepare your proposal application. A single application has two components - an Online Cover Sheet and a Project Outline.

   Online Cover Sheet       KECK ONLINE COVER SHEET
The Keck Observatory requires an online cover sheet that is available at the above link. You will need to create a Keck user account if you do not already have one. Please make sure to include ALL collaborators on this cover sheet, not just those from Swinburne. As part of the cover sheet, observers are instructed to indicate which observing run dates during the semester are preferred (P), acceptable (A), or not acceptable (X) for their programs. It is to the observer's advantage to be as liberal as possible in specifying acceptable dates. A PDF version of the Keck cover sheet is to be sent with the project outline. Using your web browser's print facility, this cover sheet can be saved as a PDF file or postscript and converted to pdf using the UNIX ps2pdf command.

   Project Outline                STACK PROPOSAL FORM
All applicants will need to download the 2013 version of the STACK proposal form above. You will need to read the instructions for each section in the latex file (they are commented out as not to appear on the final proposal).
There have been changes to the instructions for this semester, but the overall format of the proposal remains the same. The project outline should be no more than 6-pages (including figures and references) with a minimum of 12pt font as detailed in the pro-forma. The Scientific Justification section can be up to a total of three pages including all figures, tables and references. A single additional page should be used for the Technical Justification and Flexibility & Minimum time sections and for the Target list. Up to two additional pages can be used for the remaining items (and long target lists). Colour images are accepted, however they should not be so large that the files cannot be emailed. The project outline must be saved in PDF format.

(4) Combine the two PDF files into one file. This can be done in a variety of ways such as using the program combinepdfs or dragging and dropping in preview for Macs or by using the UNIX pdfmerge command. Proposals must use the naming convention PIsurname_Keckproposalnumber.pdf (e.g. Cooke_2013B_W056LA.pdf) and emailed to the STACK technical tecretary (jcooke@astro.swin.edu.au) by the deadline. The Keck proposal number can be found at the top of the Keck Cover Sheet and is issued once you fill out and save an initial version of the Cover Sheet.


Submitting a Proposal for Caltech Collaborative Time

In the interests of promoting collaborative research between Swinburne and Caltech, collaborations are to jointly apply to both of their respective time allocation committees for Keck time for joint projects on an approximately equal time basis. If the proposed joint project is not supported by both time allocation committees, the proposed project will receive zero nights from either so that no party is disadvantaged and each time allocation committee has a clear incentive to support their scientists when a genuine desire to collaborate exists. Caltech will include a summary of Collaborative Time allocations so PIs can make collaborative allocations informed by the program history. The link to this summary will be provided here after this semester.

Swinburne and Caltech collaborations will need to apply to the STACK following the guidelines described on this webpage and directly to the Caltech TAC here. Caltech Collaborative time proposals are due by 17:00 PDT, 2 April, 2013.

STACK policies and Procedures


Proposal Outcomes

The STACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-October (semester A) and late-April (semester B). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).


Remote Observing/Eavesdropping

For those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities in "eavesdropping" mode, you must contact WMKO's Mainland Observing Coordinators and Jeff Cooke in advance of your run (4 weeks for mainland-only observing, 2 weeks for mainland eavesdropping mode). Currently, fully-remote (mainland-only) observing is not available but may be in the near future.


Financial Assistance

The Swinburne Advisory Committee for Keck (SACK) proposes that applications for Keck Observing Support (KOS) funds should be made by PIs of successful proposals once they have determined the amount of funding to be available from other sources, particularly the Faculty Research Committee and AMRFP funds. After expenses have been acquitted, KOS applications can be submitted using this form in which the PI will state their total costs and the other sources of funds they have pursued. KOS applications should be sent to the STACK technical secretary after the expenses have been incurred. The technical secretary will check the reasons for the request and pass any queries to the SACK. The SACK will approve all allocations of KOS funds. The maximum KOS funding allocated to any given proposal will be $2200. At the end of each calendar year, residual funds in the Keck support budget for that year may be distributed retrospectively to that year's Keck users.
