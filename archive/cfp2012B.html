Available Time

A total of 9 Keck nights are available for 2012B (Aug 1 2012 - Jan 31 2013).
   Keck  I:   5 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 0 grey, and 4 bright
   Keck II:   4 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 0 grey, and 3 bright
The number of nights on each telescope and lunation distribution is mostly fixed, however there may be some flexibility and this will be decided by the STACK and sent to Caltech for approval. Any flexibility will be mainly in the lunation distribution on each telescope, with much less flexibility, if any, in the number of nights on each telescope.


Instrument Availability

Specific changes/new items for Semester 2012B:

- MOSFIRE will be available as a shared-risk instrument on Keck I for semester 2012B. Because this instrument has neither been installed nor tested, this unprecedented availability will require the STACK to provide Caltech/Keck with a HIRES or LRIS backup program along with their allocations for any MOSFIRE time. The HIRES or LRIS back-up programs will be matched to the MOSFIRE allocations but will require the usual sky brightness and lunation considerations.
- OSIRIS is classified as a Keck I shared-risk instrument for 2012B because it has not yet been tested on-sky with, or without, the Keck I laser.
- The Keck Interferometer will not be offered this semester.

Refer to Keck Instrument Availability webpage for details on these, and all available instruments for the 2013A semester (this page will be updated soon).

Laser Guide Star Observations

Laser Guide Star (LGS) time is now available for both the KI and KII telescopes. The restriction on the number of available LGS nights has been lifted. Keck would like scientific demand from all institutions to determine both the number of nights and the distribution of nights between Keck I and Keck II.

Because half nights of laser science combined with non-laser science are almost as resource intensive for Keck as whole LGS nights, proposers are encouraged to match half night LGS allocations with other LGS allocations. The STACK will note that the Keck I and Keck II lasers will not be scheduled on the same night unless it is for a program doing simultaneous observations on both telescopes and the STACK will take care to distribute allocated laser nights across the semester to avoid over-burdening the laser support team in any given month.


Submitting a Proposal

(1) Check your eligibility in the STACK policies and Procedures document.

(2) Check instrument availability and latest information. Details of Keck instruments available in 2013A are given on the Keck Instrument Availability page. Instrument specifics and relevant proposal information can be found on the Keck Instruments & Facilties page. For example, LRIS and DEIMOS slitmasks are due at least 4 weeks prior to the observations.

(3) Prepare your proposal application. A single application has two components - an online cover sheet and project outline.

   Online Cover Sheet - The Keck Observatory requires an online cover sheet that is available here (this requires you to create a Keck user account). Please make sure to include ALL your collaborators on this cover sheet, not just those from Swinburne. As part of the cover sheet, observers are instructed to indicate which observing run dates during the semester are preferred (P), acceptable (A), or not acceptable (X) for their programs. It is to the observer's advantage to be as liberal as possible in specifying acceptable runs. A PDF version of the Keck cover sheet is to be sent with the project outline. Using your web browser's print facility, this cover sheet can be saved as a PDF file or postscript and converted to pdf using the UNIX ps2pdf command.

   Project Outline - A project outline (the proposal) must be submitted following the headlines given in the pro-forma latex file available here. The project outline should be no more than 6-pages (including figures and references) with a minimum of 12pt font as detailed in the pro-forma. The Scientific Justification section can be up to a total of three pages including all figures, tables and references. A single additional page should be used for the Technical Justification and Flexibility & Minimum time sections and for the Target list. Up to two additional pages can be used for the remaining items (and long target lists). Colour images are accepted, however they should not be so large that the files cannot be emailed. The project outline must be saved in PDF format.

(4) Combine the two PDF files into one file. This can be done in a variety of ways such as using the program combinepdfs or dragging and dropping in preview for Macs or by using the UNIX pdfmerge command. Proposals must use the naming convention PIsurname_semester_instrument.pdf (e.g. Kacprzak_13A_DEIMOS.pdf) and emailed to the STACK technical secretary (jcooke@astro.swin.edu.au) by the deadline.

Submitting a Proposal for Caltech Collaborative Time

Swinburne researchers also have access to 5 nights a semester via collaboration with researchers at Caltech. To access this time, the Caltech staff member must be the PI of the proposal and you will need to apply directly to the Caltech TAC here. Caltech Collaborative time proposals are due by 17:00 PDT, October 1, 2012.

STACK policies and Procedures


Proposal Outcomes

The STACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-October (semester A) and late-April (semester B). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).


Remote Observing/Eavesdropping

For those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities in "eavesdropping" mode, you must contact WMKO's Mainland Observing Coordinators and Jeff Cooke in advance of your run (4 weeks for mainland-only observing, 2 weeks for mainland eavesdropping mode). Currently, fully-remote (mainland-only) observing is not available but may be in the near future.


Financial Assistance

The Swinburne Advisory Committee for Keck (SACK) proposes that applications for Keck Observing Support (KOS) funds should be made by PIs of successful proposals once they have determined the amount of funding to be available from other sources, particularly the Faculty Research Committee and AMRFP funds. After expenses have been acquitted, KOS applications can be submitted using this form in which the PI will state their total costs and the other sources of funds they have pursued. KOS applications should be sent to the STACK technical secretary after the expenses have been incurred. The technical secretary will check the reasons for the request and pass any queries to the SACK. The SACK will approve all allocations of KOS funds. The maximum KOS funding allocated to any given proposal will be $2200. At the end of each calendar year, residual funds in the Keck support budget for that year may be distributed retrospectively to that year's Keck users.
