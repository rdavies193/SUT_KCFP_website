The primary role of the Swinburne Time Allocation Committee for Keck (STACK) is to ensure the maximum scientific outcomes for Swinburne from time on the Keck telescopes. See here STACK P&P for details.

General information about applying for Keck time can be found on the Keck Observatory Website here. This is the Keck website that includes people to contact if you have questions, forms, observing considerations, and information for Keck proposals for other institutions.


Available Time

A total of 6 Keck nights are available for 2019B (August 1, 2019 - January 31, 2020).

   Keck  I:   2 nights are available with lunation distribution 0 dark, 1 grey, 1 bright
   Keck II:   4 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 1 grey, 2 bright

The number of nights on each telescope and lunation distribution is mostly fixed, however there may be some flexibility and this will be decided by the STACK and sent to Caltech for approval. Any flexibility will be mainly in the lunation distribution on each telescope, with much less flexibility, if any, in the number of nights on each telescope.


Director’s Discretionary Time (DDT)

In accordance with the Caltech and Swinburne collaborative agreement for Keck, the COO and SUT Directors have agreed to set aside, between them, 1-2 nights each semester from their Director’s allocation to support highly ranked CIT-SUT collaborative proposals. These proposals should seek to develop new and innovative scientific collaborations esp. those with long term strategic potential to have high scientific impact and/or increase the research capabilities of each organisation. The following additional selection criteria will be used for evaluation:

(1) High-risk high-reward pilot proposals for new science
(2) pilot for strategic collaborative project
(3) significant ongoing collaboration potential
(4) evidence of existing significant ongoing collaboration

Applicants may use up to 0.5 additional pages in the proposal template to discuss how their proposal meets all or any of these selection criteria. The TACs will make a recommendation on these to the Directors. The final decision on these allocations will be made by the COO and SUT Directors jointly. They reserve the right not to make an allocation if proposals do not reach a high enough bar.


Target of Opportunity (ToO)

Swinburne scheduled time may now be interrupted by the WMKO ToO program. Each hour of interrupted ToO time is compensated by 2 hours of observing time. Please see STACK P&P for details. After a call for expressions of interest and careful consideration by the SACK, we are not pursuing ToO triggers in Swinburne call for proposals this semester.


Instrument Availability

Refer to Keck Instrument Availability webpage for details on all available instruments for the 2019B semester. Pay attention to "Special Notes for Semester 2019B" on that webpage as it contains important updates about telescopes and instruments (e.g., AO-LGS on Keck I unavailable from Nov-Jan, impacting OSIRIS and NIRSPEC AO observations).


Laser Guide Star Observations

Laser Guide Star (LGS) time is now available for both the KI and KII telescopes.
Because half nights of laser science combined with non-laser science are almost as resource intensive for Keck as whole LGS nights, proposers are encouraged to match half night LGS allocations with other LGS allocations. The STACK will note that the Keck I and Keck II lasers will not be scheduled on the same night unless it is for a program doing simultaneous observations on both telescopes and the STACK will take care to distribute allocated laser nights across the semester to avoid over-burdening the laser support team in any given month.

AO Upgrades: The new LGS near-IR tip/tilt system on Keck I, TRICK is now available. Refer to Keck AO page and this document.



Submitting a Proposal:

(1) Check your eligibility in the Applicant Policy and Procedure document.

(2) Check instrument availability and latest information. Details of Keck instruments available in 2019B are given on the Keck Instrument Availability page. Instrument specifics and relevant proposal information can be found on the Keck Instruments & Facilties page. For example, LRIS and DEIMOS slitmasks are due at least 4 weeks prior to the observations.

(3) Prepare your proposal application. A single application has two components - an Online Cover Sheet and a Project Outline.

   Online Cover Sheet       KECK ONLINE COVER SHEET
The Keck Observatory requires an online cover sheet that is available at the above link. You will need to create a Keck user account if you do not already have one. Please make sure to include ALL collaborators on this cover sheet, not just those from Swinburne. As part of the cover sheet, observers are instructed to indicate which observing run dates during the semester are preferred (P), acceptable (A), or not acceptable (X) for their programs. It is to the observer's advantage to be as liberal as possible in specifying acceptable dates. A PDF version of the Keck cover sheet is to be sent with the project outline. Using your web browser's print facility, this cover sheet can be saved as a PDF file or postscript and converted to pdf using the UNIX ps2pdf command.

   Project Outline                STACK PROPOSAL FORM
All applicants will need to download the 2019B version of the STACK proposal form above. You will need to read the instructions for each section in the latex file (they are commented out as not to appear on the final proposal).
There have been changes to the instructions for this semester, and the overall format of the proposal has been re-ordered. The full project outline should be written in a minimum of 12pt font as detailed in the pro-forma. The Scientific Justification section can be up to a total of 3 pages for text and for figures, tables and references. A single additional page should be used for the Technical Justification and Flexibility & Minimum time sections. Up to two additional pages can be used for the remaining sections. Target lists and the sections Previous STACK Allocation Outcomes are to be attached to the end of the proposal. These attached pages do not count toward the total page count but cannot include any other information. Colour images are accepted, however the image files should not be so large that the proposals cannot be emailed (i.e., proposals should be less than 1MB in total, but exceptions can be made). The project outline must be saved in PDF format.

(4) Combine the two PDF files into one file. This can be done in a variety of ways such as using the program combinepdfs or dragging and dropping in preview for Macs or by using the UNIX pdfmerge command. Proposals must use the naming convention PIlastname_semesterAB_proposalnumber.pdf (e.g. Lastname_2019B_W180.pdf) and emailed to the STACK technical tecretary (tiantianyuan@swin.edu.au) by the deadline. The Keck proposal number can be found at the top of the Keck Cover Sheet and is issued once you fill out and save an initial version of the Cover Sheet.

(5) For PIs that have been awarded time in the past, you will need to describe your outcomes on the Keck Publication Page here.

Proposal Outcomes

The STACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-October (semester A) and late-April (semester B). Approved proposals are not confirmed until Keck is able to successfully schedule the time (which is not guaranteed). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).


Remote Observing/Eavesdropping - Fully remote observing is now operational! Keck Remote Control Room

Swinburne now has the capability to control the instruments and observations at Keck completely remotely from campus (room Luton Lane 2L003), i.e., mainland-only observation mode. Duplicate network connections, a backup ISDN line, duplicate (mirrored) computers, and an uninterrupted power supply are a few of the features added to ensure trouble-free observations. The fully remote capability complements Swinburne's long-standing remote eavesdropping mode. For remote observing assistance, please contact Rob Bassett (rbassett@swin.edu.au)

Those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities for either fully remote or eavesdropping mode, must contact Keck's Mainland Observing Coordinators and Rob Bassett (rbassett@swin.edu.au). This must be done 4 weeks in advance of your run for mainland fully remote mode and 2 weeks in advance for mainland eavesdropping mode.
