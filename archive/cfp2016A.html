Submitting a Proposal for Swinburne Keck Time
Swinburne is now combining the proposal assessment and time allocation process with both ANU and AAL. Proposals for Swinburne Keck time must be submitted to the Austrailian Keck TAC (KTAC). You can find more information about submitting a proposal to KTAC here. Prior to the KTAC meeting, the SACK will meet to ensure that all proposals for Swinburne time are compliant with Swinburne criteria for use of Keck time. Check your eligibility for Swinburne Keck time in the Applicant Policy and Procedure document. Note that students are not allowed to PI programs asking for Swinburne time.

When will I know the outcome of the proposal? KTACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-November (semester A) and late-April (semester B). Approved proposals are not confirmed until Keck is able to successfully schedule the time (which is not guaranteed). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).

Remote Observing/Eavesdropping - Fully remote observing is now operational!

Swinburne now has the capability to control the instruments and observations at Keck completely remotely from campus (room SA 118), i.e., mainland-only observation mode. Duplicate network connections, a backup ISDN line, duplicate (mirrored) computers, and an uninterrupted power supply are a few of the features added to ensure trouble-free observations. The fully remote capability complements Swinburne's long-standing remote eavesdropping mode.

Those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities for either fully remote or eavesdropping mode, must contact Keck's Mainland Observing Coordinators and David Fisher. This must be done 4 weeks in advance of your run for mainland fully remote mode and 2 weeks in advance for mainland eavesdropping mode.

Financial Assistance for Travel to Keck
The ITSO travel policy currently supports 1 inexperienced person (defined as not having used that instrument within the last 2 years) to attend a Keck run. SACK will fund 1 person unless ITSO are funding a Swinburne postdoc or a staff member. So SACK will ensure that at least 1 person is funded to attend the run. The normal limit for travel support is $2K per run. Exceptions to any of above can be requested by making a case to the Chair of the SACK, who will normally consult with other SACK members. Feel free to contact the SACK Chair before filling out the Activiti form to discuss the funding of your upcoming Keck run. The travel funding situation is both fluid and has limited funds, and so is subject to change.
