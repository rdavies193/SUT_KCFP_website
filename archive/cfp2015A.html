Available Time

A total of 8 Keck nights are available for 2015A (February 1 2015 - July 31 2015).

   Keck  I:   5 nights are available with lunation distribution 0 dark, 3 grey, 2 bright
   Keck II:   3 nights are available with lunation distribution 1 dark, 1 grey, 1 bright

The number of nights on each telescope and lunation distribution is mostly fixed, however there may be some flexibility and this will be decided by the STACK and sent to Caltech for approval. Any flexibility will be mainly in the lunation distribution on each telescope, with much less flexibility, if any, in the number of nights on each telescope.


Instrument Availability

Refer to Keck Instrument Availability webpage for details on all available instruments for the 2015A semester.


Laser Guide Star Observations

Laser Guide Star (LGS) time is now available for both the KI and KII telescopes.
Because half nights of laser science combined with non-laser science are almost as resource intensive for Keck as whole LGS nights, proposers are encouraged to match half night LGS allocations with other LGS allocations. The STACK will note that the Keck I and Keck II lasers will not be scheduled on the same night unless it is for a program doing simultaneous observations on both telescopes and the STACK will take care to distribute allocated laser nights across the semester to avoid over-burdening the laser support team in any given month.

AO Upgrades: The new near-IR tip/tilt system on Keck I, TRICK, might be available in 15A on a shared-risk basis. However, proposals should not rely on its availability. In addition, the laser launch on Keck II might be switched to the new center launch system (CLS), providing the AO system with a smaller laser spot size.


Submitting a Proposal

(1) Check your eligibility in the Applicant Policy and Procedure document.

(2) Check instrument availability and latest information. Details of Keck instruments available in 2014B are given on the Keck Instrument Availability page. Instrument specifics and relevant proposal information can be found on the Keck Instruments & Facilties page. For example, LRIS and DEIMOS slitmasks are due at least 4 weeks prior to the observations.

(3) Prepare your proposal application. A single application has two components - an Online Cover Sheet and a Project Outline.

   Online Cover Sheet       KECK ONLINE COVER SHEET
The Keck Observatory requires an online cover sheet that is available at the above link. You will need to create a Keck user account if you do not already have one. Please make sure to include ALL collaborators on this cover sheet, not just those from Swinburne. As part of the cover sheet, observers are instructed to indicate which observing run dates during the semester are preferred (P), acceptable (A), or not acceptable (X) for their programs. It is to the observer's advantage to be as liberal as possible in specifying acceptable dates. A PDF version of the Keck cover sheet is to be sent with the project outline. Using your web browser's print facility, this cover sheet can be saved as a PDF file or postscript and converted to pdf using the UNIX ps2pdf command.

   Project Outline                STACK PROPOSAL FORM
All applicants will need to download the 2015A version of the STACK proposal form above. You will need to read the instructions for each section in the latex file (they are commented out as not to appear on the final proposal).
There have been changes to the instructions for this semester, and the overall format of the proposal has been re-ordered. The full project outline should be written in a minimum of 12pt font as detailed in the pro-forma. The Scientific Justification section can be up to a total of 3 pages, including figures, tables and references. A single additional page should be used for the Technical Justification and Flexibility & Minimum time sections. Up to two additional pages can be used for the remaining sections. Target lists and the sections Previous STACK Allocation Outcomes are to be attached to the end of the proposal. These attached pages do not count toward the total page count but cannot include any other information. Colour images are accepted, however the image files should not be so large that the proposals cannot be emailed (i.e., proposals should be less than 1MB in total, but exceptions can be made). The project outline must be saved in PDF format.

(4) Combine the two PDF files into one file. This can be done in a variety of ways such as using the program combinepdfs or dragging and dropping in preview for Macs or by using the UNIX pdfmerge command. Proposals must use the naming convention PIusername_semester_proposalnumber.pdf (e.g. Cooke_2015A_W123LA.pdf) and emailed to the STACK technical tecretary (jcooke@astro.swin.edu.au) by the deadline. The Keck proposal number can be found at the top of the Keck Cover Sheet and is issued once you fill out and save an initial version of the Cover Sheet.

(5) For PIs that have been awarded time in the past, you will need to describe your outcomes on the Keck Publication Page here.


Submitting a Proposal for Caltech Collaborative Time

In the interests of promoting collaborative research between Swinburne and Caltech, collaborations are to jointly apply to both of their respective time allocation committees for Keck time for joint projects on an approximately equal time basis. If the proposed joint project is not supported by both time allocation committees, the proposed project will receive zero nights from either so that no party is disadvantaged and each time allocation committee has a clear incentive to support their scientists when a genuine desire to collaborate exists. Caltech will include a summary of Collaborative Time allocations so PIs can make collaborative allocations informed by the program history. The link to this summary will be provided here after this semester.

Swinburne and Caltech collaborations will need to apply to the STACK following the guidelines described on this webpage and directly to the Caltech TAC here. Caltech Collaborative time proposals need to be sent to Robert Brucato (brucato@astro.caltech.edu) and are due by 18:00 PDT, 01 October, 2014.

Proposal Outcomes

The STACK will meet in early October to review semester "A" proposals and in early April to review semester "B" proposals. The STACK then puts forth their recommendations to Caltech. Proposers will learn of the results late-October (semester A) and late-April (semester B). Approved proposals are not confirmed until Keck is able to successfully schedule the time (which is not guaranteed). The Keck telescope schedule is released on 1 December (semester A) and 1 June (semester B).


Remote Observing/Eavesdropping - Fully remote observing is now operational!

Swinburne now has the capability to control the instruments and observations at Keck completely remotely from campus (room SA 118), i.e., mainland-only observation mode. Duplicate network connections, a backup ISDN line, duplicate (mirrored) computers, and an uninterrupted power supply are a few of the features added to ensure trouble-free observations. The fully remote capability complements Swinburne's long-standing remote eavesdropping mode.

Those who wish to use the Swinburne remote facilities for either fully remote or eavesdropping mode, must contact Keck's Mainland Observing Coordinators and Jeff Cooke. This must be done 4 weeks in advance of your run for mainland fully remote mode and 2 weeks in advance for mainland eavesdropping mode.


Financial Assistance

After the Keck schedule is released, applications should be made to Swinburne Research for financial support via the Access to Major Research Facilities Scheme (AMRFS). This scheme was made possible from efforts made by Michael Murphy as FICT ADR.

The Swinburne Advisory Committee for Keck (SACK) proposes that applications for Keck Observing Support (KOS) funds should be made by PIs of successful proposals once they have determined the amount of funding to be available from other sources, particularly the Faculty Research Grant Schemes and the above-mentioned AMRFS funds. KOS funding is now integrated into the T7 travel form.

Keck users may also apply to the SACK Chair for co-funding to employ a vacation student or Research Assistant (level 1) to help accelerate the process of publishing their Keck data. The base case is that the SACK will fund half of the cost of a vacation student or RA for 8 weeks (in 2014 those costs were $4K/2 and $11K/2). It is expected that the Keck PI (or staff Co-I) fund the other half. Variations on the base case will also be considered. There is no application deadline but success is subject to funding being available. The application should make the case and give relevant details for using SACK funds within a 1 page letter or email to the SACK Chair.
